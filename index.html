About Me

My name is Jennifer Hou. I was born in Taiwan and have been living in Georgia for the past 20 years. I received my master’s degree from Purdue University and earned the Sun Java Programmer certificate upon completing a Java Coding Boot Camp in 2000. Since then, I have been working as a Java developer and participated in various application projects. In the year of 2010, I decided to stay home where my kids needed me the most. I am glad that I had the chance to be there for my children when the family activities became overwhelming.

Both of my kids were born and raised in Georgia. My son is in his 2nd year at the Medical College of Georgia and my daughter is a freshman at Cornell University. My husband serves as the director of a research and technology department. Seeing that all my family members were busy reaching goals in their lives, I too desired to have something of my own. I pushed myself to earned a certificate for medical interpretation in Mandarin last year. I was so encouraged when I passed both the written and verbal exams with flying colors. While studying for the medical interpreter training course, I realized that I am most happy when I can continue learning and I realized that my love for programming is still strong. I then decided to search up a good coding boot camp to help me sharpen my skills for all the new web technologies.

Inevitably I have fears that trying to get back to coding again. “The more I learn the more I realize the less I know.” ― Michel Legrand. That’s exactly how I feel throughout the entire boot camp course. However, I am also telling myself to think in a positive way. “You'll never know everything about anything, especially something you love.” ― Julia Child. I told myself that I should be proud that I have taken the first step out and I am taking a step at a time!
